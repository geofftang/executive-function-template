# Chief of Staff Model

The core philosophy behind this system.

---

## The Insight

**The pile is the assault, not the individual items.**

Seeing 47 emails, 12 overdue tasks, and 8 unread messages triggers ambient stress independent of any item's difficulty. The cognitive load of *knowing everything that's pending* is often worse than doing any single thing.

Most productivity systems make this worse by showing you everything. This system does the opposite: **the AI holds the pile, and you see one next thing.**

---

## UX Principles

| Instead of... | Do this... |
|---------------|------------|
| "Here's your inbox (47)" | "2 need replies, 1 urgent, rest handled" |
| "Here's your task list" | "One thing: [X]. Say 'what else' for more" |
| "You have 12 overdue" | "Morning routine done? One thing due by 5pm" |
| "Let's go through each area" | "Everything's fine except [area]. Focus there." |

**The user never sees the pile. The AI holds it.**

---

## Stuck Item Protocol

For tasks accumulating avoidance (you know you should do it, but you can't start):

1. **Break into micro-steps** (2-5 min each) — "Just open the document. That's it."
2. **Draft the hard thing** — AI writes the email, message, or form. User just reviews.
3. **Body-double** — "I'll stay while you make the call. Start when ready."
4. **Remove decision fatigue** — "Here's exactly what to do, step by step."

The goal: reduce activation energy to near-zero. Once someone starts, momentum often carries them.

---

## Autonomous vs Scaffolded

| AI does autonomously | AI scaffolds (needs user) |
|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Categorize emails by urgency | Approve email drafts |
| Surface overdue items | Decide what to tackle |
| Route captures to systems | Confirm priority assignments |
| Archive processed items | Final "send" on messages |
| Track balance across areas | Choose when to pivot |
| Detect stagnation | Decide: push through or defer |

**Rule of thumb:** AI handles information management. User makes commitments and approvals.

---

## Why "Chief of Staff"

A Chief of Staff in an organization:
- Knows everything that's happening across departments
- Filters what reaches the executive
- Prepares briefings with recommended actions
- Handles logistics so the executive focuses on decisions

This system does the same for an individual:
- Knows all your tasks, emails, calendar, projects
- Filters what you see based on priority and energy
- Prepares daily plans with one recommended task per area
- Handles categorization and routing so you focus on doing

The key shift: **you are the executive, not the administrator.** The system handles the administrative overhead of managing your life.
